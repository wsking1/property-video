Market Analysis
The Opportunity
Why this gap exists, how big it actually is, and why the timing works in your favour.
New Zealand's real estate market runs on visual marketing — and right now, most of it is photo-only. Every week, thousands of fresh listings go live on Trade Me Property without a single second of video. Agents know video sells faster and holds buyer attention longer, but the traditional path to getting it — booking a drone operator, waiting for good weather, paying several hundred dollars, and waiting up to a week for delivery — carries enough friction that most agents simply skip it.
That gap is the entire opportunity. You're not trying to convince agents that video matters — the industry already knows that. You're removing the three things stopping them from using it: cost, speed, and hassle. AI-generated property video collapses a $600, one-week process into a $180, 48-hour one — no drone licence, no travel, no weather risk. For an agent on a $780,000 listing, that's a marketing decision that costs less than a tank of petrol.

The Psychology Behind Every Yes
Understanding why an agent says yes — and why they hesitate — is the difference between a generic pitch and one that actually converts.
💰
Cost Anchoring
Agents already have a mental price tag for property video — usually $400 to $900, based on what drone operators charge. When you quote $150–$280, you're not asking them to spend money on something new; you're offering the same category of service at a third of the expected price. That gap alone does most of the selling for you.
⏱️
Speed Wins Listings
The first 7–10 days a listing is live get the most views and enquiries — momentum sets the tone for the whole campaign. A drone booking that takes a week often arrives after that window has closed. A 48-hour AI video means the property goes to market with full marketing assets from day one, not as an afterthought two weeks in.
🎯
Competitive Differentiation
Open any street in a competitive suburb and you'll see five or six listings side by side, all using the same static photo format. The one with a cinematic video stands out instantly in a buyer's scroll. Agents are acutely aware they're competing with the listing next door — video is the cheapest premium upgrade available to them.
📲
The Social Media Push
Every agency now expects its agents to post listings to Instagram and Facebook. A static photo carousel performs poorly against video content on every major platform's algorithm. Because your service includes a ready-made vertical cut, you're solving a problem agents already feel pressure to solve — without them having to ask.
From 35,000 Listings to Your First Client
It helps to think about this opportunity as a funnel rather than a single number. Start with the roughly 35,000 active residential listings on Trade Me Property at any given time. Strip out the ones that already have video — that still leaves tens of thousands without it. Of those, a meaningful share won't be a fit: very low-value listings where a few hundred dollars genuinely doesn't make sense for the agent's margin, or properties visually unremarkable enough that no amount of cinematic treatment changes the pitch.
What's left is still substantial. Narrow the funnel down to listings priced above $400,000, with reasonable photo quality, listed in the last 30 days, with an identifiable and contactable agent — and conservative estimates still put your realistic weekly pitch pool in the hundreds. You don't need to convert a large percentage of this pool to build meaningful income. At a 10–15% reply rate and a portion of those converting to paid work, consistent daily outreach produces a steady stream of clients without ever running dry on leads.

Who You're Really Competing With
It's tempting to think of drone videographers as your main competitor, but in practice, the agent's true default option is doing nothing at all. The vast majority of NZ listings ship with photos only — no video, no virtual tour, nothing beyond static images and a written description. That's not because agents don't see the value in better marketing; it's because the existing routes to getting video all carry enough friction that most agents simply never get around to it.
🚁 Drone & videography operators
Real competitors, but priced and positioned for the top 10–15% of high-value listings. Most regional and mid-market properties are priced out of this option entirely — exactly your space.
😴 Doing nothing (the true default)
Your biggest competitor isn't a company, it's inertia. Most agents have simply never been offered a fast, cheap alternative — which is why a single demo video often does more convincing than any sales pitch ever could.
🤖 Other AI video providers
As AI property video tools mature, more entrants will appear in this space over time. The advantage goes to whoever builds real relationships and a reputation first — which is exactly what early, consistent outreach is designed to secure.
Why Now Is the Right Time
Markets like this don't stay wide open forever. AI video generation has only become genuinely usable for real estate-grade output in the last year or two — the technology simply wasn't good enough before that to produce something an agent would be comfortable putting in front of a vendor. At the same time, awareness among NZ real estate agents of AI-generated content is still low compared to markets like the US and Australia, where agencies have already begun adopting it more broadly.
This creates a window: the tools are mature enough to produce genuinely impressive results, but the market hasn't caught up yet. The agents you pitch in the next few months are very likely seeing something like this for the first time. That novelty works heavily in your favour — both for getting attention in a crowded inbox, and for being remembered as the person who introduced them to it, rather than just one of several competing options later on.
Key Market Facts
Trade Me flags video listings+
Trade Me Property explicitly labels listings that have video tours. Listings WITHOUT video are identifiable in under 5 seconds. You have a daily, renewable lead pool of 25,000–28,000 properties at any given time.
NZ agents are behind the curve+
AI property video is mainstream in Australia and the US. NZ agents are 2–3 years behind. Early movers face minimal competition and high novelty value — the demo video alone creates curiosity that opens doors.
Video listings get more engagement+
Property listings with video content consistently generate higher click-through and longer time-on-listing than photo-only listings. Whether a buyer ultimately purchases the home or not, increased engagement matters to agents because it signals strong marketing performance to the vendor — the homeowner who hired them. Agents aren't just selling houses; they're constantly proving their value to the people who pay their commission. A video that performs well is evidence they can point to.
Agents already have everything you need+
Unlike a drone operator who needs to physically attend the property, your raw material — high-quality professional photography — already exists. Every listing on Trade Me comes with a full photo set taken by a photographer the agent has already paid for. You're not asking the agent to organise anything new. You just need photos that are already public and freely available, which means your time-to-delivery is limited only by your own production speed — not by waiting on access, weather, or scheduling.
The real cost of doing nothing+
When an agent declines a video, there's rarely an explicit downside they feel immediately — the listing still goes up, photos still get taken, open homes still happen. This is exactly why most agents have never been pushed hard enough to solve the problem; the cost of not having video is invisible rather than painful. Your job in a pitch isn't to manufacture urgency — it's to make the upside visible by showing them, with a real demo of their actual listing, exactly what they're currently leaving on the table.
Why the first "yes" compounds+
Real estate is a referral-dense industry. Agents within the same office regularly compare notes on marketing vendors, and principals actively look for ways to give their whole team a competitive edge. A single satisfied agent rarely stays a single client for long — they mention you in the office, forward your details to a colleague with a similar listing, or simply keep coming back every time they sign a new vendor. The economics of this business reward patience with the first few clients far more than they reward volume of cold outreach alone.
What Could Slow This Down
No honest opportunity is risk-free. Here's what's genuinely worth being aware of.
⚡ AI scepticism
Some agents will be instinctively wary of anything labelled "AI generated," particularly more senior or traditional operators. The fix is simple: lead with the demo video itself rather than the technology behind it. Most people respond to what they see, not how it was made.
📈 Market awareness will grow
As more people in NZ realise this is possible, competition will increase over time. This is exactly why building real relationships and reputation now — rather than relying purely on cold volume later — is the highest-leverage use of your early effort.
🖼️ Quality variance
Not every photo set produces an equally strong result. Listings with poor or very few photos will be harder to turn into compelling video. Part of lead qualification (covered in the Workflow tab) exists specifically to filter for properties where the raw material gives you a real chance of an excellent result.

Thousands of NZ listings go to market every month with no video, agents already understand why video matters, and the only thing standing between them and a polished cinematic clip is cost, speed, and habit. You've already removed two of those barriers — the third only needs one demo video to break.
